I enjoy software development, because it's a mix of creativity and engineering.

I'm not one of the developers who started coding at an early age and loved math. Instead, I invested a great deal of my time in learning foreign languages, especially German and English.

In 2019 I graduated Heilbronn University Of Applied Sciences with a B.Sc. in Computer Science. In retrospect, I really enjoyed my four years there, although they were sometimes anything but easy. During this time I also completed an internship at a company called CAS Software AG. The product that I worked on was a big enterprise web application written entirely in Java EE. I learned how to navigate through a huge codebase, to debug code and to write tests. After graduating from college I moved myself and everything I owned to Munich and joined MaibornWolff where I work as a mobile application developer.

My first project was a health care app for Android written in Kotlin. Neither was my Android knowledge proficient, nor have I had any previous Kotlin experience, so I had to pick both up quickly and also learn the app architecture. Nevertheless, in the context of a specific feature I designed it's data models, added the Room persistence library, worked with background task execution at a specific time, added notifications. I even implemented a separate module for tracking network type and speed as well as a widget showing a gallery of videos and playing them. Overall, it was a success.

In mid 2020 I joined BMW's Mobile Connected project where I started learning and working with Dart and Flutter. This experience was particularly useful as I gained knowledge of how to communicate in an international large scale project. I single handedly implemented a small feature (front- and backend), massively shaped not only the i18n of the app, but also made it more accessible by adding semantics for screen readers like VoiceOver.

Today I plan on working with TypeScript and a couple of other useful web languages and technologies. My story is to be continued.
